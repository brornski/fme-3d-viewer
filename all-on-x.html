Full Arch Solution

All-on-X Full Mouth Implants

All-on-X replaces an entire arch of missing or failing teeth with just 4-6 strategically placed dental implants. A complete set of fixed, non-removable prosthetic teeth is attached — restoring your smile, chewing ability, and confidence. Many patients receive their new teeth on the same day as surgery.

How All-on-X Works

Consultation & 3D Planning

Advanced CBCT 3D imaging maps your jawbone anatomy to plan optimal implant positioning. We create a surgical guide for precise, predictable implant placement.

Strategic Implant Placement

4-6 titanium implants are placed at strategic angles to maximize bone contact. Angled posterior implants engage stronger bone areas, often eliminating the need for bone grafting.

Same-Day Temporary Teeth

In many cases, a temporary fixed prosthesis is attached on the same day as surgery. You leave the office with a full set of functional, natural-looking teeth.

Permanent Prosthesis

After 3-6 months of healing, the temporary is replaced with a permanent zirconia or high-grade acrylic prosthesis — your final, long-lasting set of beautiful teeth.

Benefits of All-on-X

Fixed, Non-Removable Teeth

Unlike dentures, All-on-X teeth are permanently fixed to implants. No adhesives, no slipping, no removing at night. They function exactly like natural teeth.

Fewer Implants, Full Results

Traditional full-arch restoration might require 8-10 implants. All-on-X achieves the same result with just 4-6, reducing surgery time, cost, and healing.

Works with Bone Loss

Angled implants engage available bone in the anterior jaw, often eliminating the need for bone grafting. Patients with significant bone loss who were told they can't get implants may still qualify.

Immediate Function

Many patients receive functional teeth the same day as surgery. Eat soft foods within days, resume a normal diet within weeks — no months of living without teeth.

All-on-X vs. Alternatives

All-on-X vs. Dentures

Dentures are removable, can slip during eating and speaking, and accelerate bone loss. All-on-X teeth are fixed, stable, and preserve jawbone through implant stimulation.

All-on-X vs. Individual Implants

Replacing every tooth with individual implants (8-14 per arch) costs significantly more. All-on-X achieves a full-arch result with just 4-6 implants at a fraction of the cost.

All-on-4 vs. All-on-6

All-on-4 uses 4 implants per arch; All-on-6 uses 6 for extra stability. Your doctor recommends the optimal number based on bone density, arch shape, and bite force requirements.

Long-Term Value

While All-on-X has a higher upfront cost than dentures, the implants can last a lifetime. Dentures need replacement every 5-8 years, making All-on-X more cost-effective over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does All-on-X cost?

All-on-X typically costs $20,000-$40,000 per arch depending on materials and complexity. We accept 40+ insurance plans and offer flexible financing options.

Can I get new teeth in one day?

Yes. Many All-on-X cases allow same-day temporary teeth — you leave the office with a full set of functional teeth. Permanent prosthesis is placed after 3-6 months of healing.

All-on-4 vs. All-on-6?

All-on-4 uses 4 implants, All-on-6 uses 6 for extra stability. Your doctor will recommend the best option based on your bone density and needs.

What if I have bone loss?

All-on-X is designed for bone loss cases. Angled implants engage available bone, often eliminating the need for bone grafting. Most patients qualify even with significant bone loss.

How long do they last?

The titanium implants can last a lifetime. The prosthetic teeth typically last 15-20 years before needing replacement. Regular maintenance visits ensure long-term success.

Can I eat normally?

Yes. After initial healing (soft foods for a few weeks), All-on-X patients eat a fully normal diet — steak, apples, corn on the cob. No food restrictions like with dentures.
