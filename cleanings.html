Preventive Dentistry

Dental Cleanings & Checkups

Regular dental cleanings and checkups are the foundation of a healthy smile. Our comprehensive visits include professional plaque and tartar removal, gentle polishing, X-rays, oral cancer screening, and a thorough exam by your dentist — all designed to catch problems early and keep your teeth and gums healthy for life.

What to Expect at Your Visit

Professional Cleaning

Our dental hygienists carefully remove plaque and tartar buildup from all tooth surfaces using specialized instruments. Teeth are polished to remove surface stains and flossed thoroughly.

Comprehensive Exam

Your dentist performs a thorough examination checking for cavities, gum disease, bite problems, and signs of oral cancer. Early detection prevents small issues from becoming major treatments.

Digital X-Rays

Low-radiation digital X-rays reveal what's hidden beneath the surface — cavities between teeth, bone loss, impacted teeth, and other concerns not visible during a visual exam.

Personalized Care Plan

Your dentist reviews all findings with you and provides personalized recommendations for home care, treatment needs, and optimal cleaning frequency based on your oral health.

Why Regular Cleanings Matter

Prevent Cavities

Professional cleaning removes plaque and tartar that brushing alone cannot eliminate. Tartar buildup leads to tooth decay — regular cleanings are your best defense against cavities.

Prevent Gum Disease

Gum disease (gingivitis and periodontitis) is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. Regular cleanings remove bacteria below the gumline that cause inflammation and bone loss.

Early Detection

Regular checkups catch problems early when they're simplest and least expensive to treat. A small cavity found today prevents a root canal or extraction tomorrow.

Overall Health Connection

Oral health is linked to heart disease, diabetes, and other systemic conditions. Regular dental care supports your whole-body health and can detect early signs of systemic disease.

Our Cleaning Services

Routine Prophylaxis

Standard cleaning for patients with healthy gums. Plaque and tartar removal, polishing, and flossing. Recommended every 6 months to maintain optimal oral health.

Deep Cleaning (Scaling & Root Planing)

Therapeutic cleaning for patients with gum disease. Removes bacteria and tartar below the gumline and smooths root surfaces to help gums reattach to teeth.

Periodontal Maintenance

Follow-up cleanings every 3-4 months for patients who have completed gum disease treatment. Maintains gum health and prevents recurrence of periodontal disease.

Fluoride Treatment

Professional fluoride application strengthens tooth enamel and helps prevent cavities. Especially beneficial for patients with a history of decay or sensitive teeth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a dental cleaning cost?

Routine cleanings cost $75-$200 without insurance. Most dental plans cover two cleanings per year at 100%. We accept 40+ insurance plans and offer competitive self-pay rates.

How often should I get a cleaning?

Every 6 months for most patients. Those with gum disease or higher risk may need cleanings every 3-4 months. Your dentist will recommend the right frequency for you.

Does dental cleaning hurt?

Routine cleanings should not be painful. You may feel mild pressure or sensitivity with heavy tartar buildup. We offer topical numbing gel for patients with sensitivity.

What is the difference between cleaning and deep cleaning?

A routine cleaning is preventive, removing buildup above the gumline. A deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) treats gum disease by removing bacteria and tartar below the gumline.

Does insurance cover cleanings?

Most dental insurance covers two preventive cleanings per year at 100% with no copay. We accept 40+ plans including Aetna, Cigna, Delta Dental, MetLife, and United Healthcare.

What should I do between cleanings?

Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, floss daily, use mouthwash, limit sugary foods, and avoid tobacco. Good home care between visits keeps your teeth and gums healthy.
