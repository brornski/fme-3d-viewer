Permanent Solution

Dental Implants

Dental implants are the gold standard for replacing missing teeth. A titanium post is surgically placed into the jawbone, where it fuses permanently through osseointegration. Once healed, a custom porcelain crown is attached — creating a restoration that looks, feels, and functions exactly like a natural tooth. With a 95-98% success rate, implants can last a lifetime.

The Dental Implant Process

Consultation & 3D Imaging

Comprehensive exam with CBCT 3D scan to evaluate bone density, map anatomy, and create your personalized implant treatment plan. We assess candidacy and discuss all options.

Implant Placement

The titanium implant post is surgically placed into the jawbone under local anesthesia. The procedure is minimally invasive with most patients returning to normal activities the next day.

Osseointegration (Healing)

Over 3-6 months, the titanium implant fuses with your natural bone through osseointegration. This creates an incredibly strong foundation that mimics a natural tooth root.

Crown Placement

An abutment is attached to the implant, followed by a custom porcelain crown matched to your natural tooth color and shape. The result is indistinguishable from your natural teeth.

Types of Dental Implants

Single Tooth Implant

Replace one missing tooth with a single implant and crown. Preserves adjacent healthy teeth — unlike bridges, no neighboring teeth need to be modified.

Multiple Tooth Implants

Replace several missing teeth with implant-supported bridges. Two or more implants support a bridge of connected crowns for a seamless, natural-looking result.

All-on-X Full Arch

Replace an entire arch of teeth with just 4-6 strategically placed implants. All-on-X provides a full set of fixed, non-removable teeth in as little as one day.

Implant-Supported Dentures

Secure loose dentures with implant attachments. Snap-on overdentures clip onto 2-4 implants for dramatically improved stability, comfort, and chewing ability.

Benefits of Dental Implants

Permanent & Long-Lasting

Dental implants are designed to last 30+ years — potentially a lifetime with proper care. Unlike dentures or bridges that need replacement every 5-15 years, implants are a one-time investment.

Preserves Jawbone

When teeth are lost, the jawbone deteriorates over time. Implants stimulate the bone just like natural tooth roots, preventing bone loss and preserving facial structure.

Natural Look & Feel

Custom porcelain crowns are matched to your natural teeth in color, shape, and translucency. Implants feel and function exactly like real teeth — eat, speak, and smile with confidence.

95-98% Success Rate

Dental implants have one of the highest success rates of any surgical procedure. Modern implant technology and materials make the procedure highly predictable and reliable.

Dental Implants vs. Alternatives

Implants vs. Dentures

Implants are fixed and permanent — no adhesives, no slipping, no removal for cleaning. Dentures are removable, can shift during eating and speaking, and accelerate bone loss over time.

Implants vs. Bridges

Implants preserve adjacent healthy teeth. Bridges require grinding down neighboring teeth for support. Implants also last significantly longer (30+ years vs. 10-15 years for bridges).

Cost vs. Value

While implants have a higher upfront cost ($3,000-$5,000 per tooth), their longevity makes them more cost-effective over time. Dentures and bridges require periodic replacement and maintenance.

Quality of Life

Implant patients report significantly higher satisfaction than denture wearers. Eat any food, speak clearly, and smile confidently without worrying about loose or shifting prosthetics.
