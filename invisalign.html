How Invisalign Works

Digital Scan & Treatment Plan

We take precise digital impressions of your teeth and create a 3D treatment plan showing every stage of your smile transformation — so you can preview your results before you start.

Custom Aligner Fabrication

Your aligners are custom-manufactured from SmartTrack material for a precise, comfortable fit. Each set is designed to make incremental adjustments to your tooth position.

Wear & Switch

Wear each set of aligners for 20-22 hours daily, switching to a new set every 1-2 weeks. Remove only for eating, drinking (non-water), and oral hygiene.

Progress Check-Ups

Visit us every 6-8 weeks for progress monitoring and to receive your next sets of aligners. Total treatment time is typically 6-18 months depending on complexity.

Benefits of Invisalign

Nearly Invisible

Clear aligners are virtually undetectable when worn. Straighten your teeth without anyone knowing — perfect for professionals, adults, and teens who want a discreet option.

Removable & Convenient

Unlike braces, Invisalign aligners are removable. Eat whatever you want, brush and floss normally, and remove for special occasions without affecting treatment.

Comfortable Fit

No metal brackets or wires means no mouth irritation. SmartTrack material provides gentle, consistent force that is more comfortable than traditional braces.

Predictable Results

Advanced 3D treatment planning lets you see your expected results before starting. Each aligner is precisely engineered for controlled, predictable tooth movement.

What Can Invisalign Treat?

Crowded Teeth

Overlapping or crowded teeth that are difficult to clean. Invisalign gradually creates space and aligns teeth for better aesthetics and oral health.

Gaps Between Teeth

Spacing issues between teeth can trap food and affect your smile. Invisalign closes gaps for a uniform, natural-looking result.

Overbite & Underbite

Mild to moderate bite misalignment can be corrected with Invisalign. For severe skeletal bite issues, we may recommend MSE or FME expansion first.

Crossbite & Open Bite

Invisalign treats crossbites where upper and lower teeth don't align properly, as well as open bites where front teeth don't meet when biting down.

Invisalign vs. Braces

Appearance

Invisalign aligners are virtually invisible. Traditional braces use metal brackets and wires that are noticeable when speaking and smiling.

Comfort

No metal means no mouth sores or irritation. Invisalign applies gentle, even pressure compared to the tightening and adjustments required with braces.

Convenience

Invisalign is removable — no food restrictions, normal brushing and flossing, and fewer office visits. Braces require dietary changes and specialized cleaning tools.

Effectiveness

Both are highly effective. Invisalign treats most mild-to-moderate cases equally well. Severe or complex cases may benefit from braces for maximum control.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Invisalign cost?

Invisalign typically costs between $3,000 and $8,000 depending on case complexity. We accept 40+ insurance plans and offer flexible financing. Many plans cover Invisalign the same as braces.

How long does Invisalign take?

Most treatments take 6-18 months. Simple cases can finish in as few as 6 months, while complex cases may take up to 18 months. We'll provide your estimated timeline at consultation.

Does insurance cover Invisalign?

Many dental insurance plans cover Invisalign like traditional braces, typically $1,500-$3,000 in benefits. We accept HSA/FSA accounts and offer in-house financing options.

How many hours a day do you wear Invisalign?

Wear aligners 20-22 hours per day for best results. Remove only for eating, drinking anything other than water, and brushing/flossing your teeth.

Is Invisalign painful?

Most patients feel mild tightness when switching to new aligners, which fades within 1-2 days. Invisalign is generally more comfortable than braces — no brackets or wires to irritate.

Can I eat with Invisalign?

Remove your aligners before eating or drinking anything other than water. There are no food restrictions — enjoy all your favorite foods, then brush before reinserting aligners.
